## The company

BikeOn builds bicycle electrification products, power meters, and supporting accessories for the e-mobility category. The hardware sits at the consumer end of climate tech: products designed to shift personal transportation away from cars and toward bikes, with manufacturing handled overseas and distribution running through both direct-to-consumer and traditional retail channels.

---

## The challenge

A few things made this engagement non-trivial. The core invention was a complex mechanical device, which meant the patent application needed careful drafting around moving parts, force transmission, and the interfaces between components. Manufacturing was in China, which made international IP rights important from day one. The budget was lean, as it usually is for hardware startups before launch.

And there was a hard deadline. BikeOn was planning to launch on Kickstarter, which is a public disclosure under patent law. The IP work needed to be on file before the campaign went live, or the team would lose the ability to file in jurisdictions where prior disclosure is disqualifying.

---

## The approach

We filed a single carefully scoped international patent application that covered the mechanical innovation across the key jurisdictions for both manufacturing protection (China) and commercial markets. The claims were drafted with an eye toward what would be defensible across borders, not just allowable in the US.

We also took over an existing patent case from BikeOn's previous counsel. That involved coordinating document transfer, reviewing the prior prosecution strategy, and picking up the work without any billing overlap or strategic discontinuity.

---

## The outcome

The international application received a positive international search report. BikeOn launched on Kickstarter and hit its funding goal quickly. The IP foundation was on file before the public disclosure, which preserved options for follow-on filings as the product line expands.

## What you can learn from this

- Kickstarter is a public disclosure. File your patents before the campaign goes live, not after.
- Manufacturing in China makes international IP strategy more important, not less. PCT applications give you a path to enforcement in the country where your products are made.
- A positive international search report is useful for both prosecution and fundraising. Investors and acquirers read those reports.
- Switching patent counsel mid-engagement is straightforward when handled correctly. We do this regularly.
